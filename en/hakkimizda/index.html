Op. Dr. Halil İbrahim Öçalan - About Us

We give you the chance to live your life pain-free with freedom of movement.

Introducing Ourselves

Op. Dr. Halil İbrahim Öçalan was born in 1984 in Kırkağaç district of Manisa and is originally from Çankırı/Çerkeş. He began his primary education in Palu district of Elazığ and continued at Yunus Emre Primary School in central Elazığ.

Dr. Öçalan, who completed his secondary education at Elazığ Anatolian High School, graduated from this school in 2002. In 2002, he entered Kırıkkale University Faculty of Medicine and pursued his medical education for 7 years with great success, receiving the title of Doctor of Medicine in 2009.

After graduation, from 2009–2011, he worked as a general practitioner in Taşlıçay district of Ağrı. He then began specialization training in Orthopedics and Traumatology in Gaziantep and became a Specialist in Orthopedics and Traumatology in 2016.

After completing his specialization training, from 2016–2023, he worked as a Specialist in Orthopedics and Traumatology at Niğde Education and Research Hospital. Since 2023, he has been continuing his professional work at Gaziantep Özel Deva Hospital.

Areas of Expertise

Op. Dr. Halil İbrahim Öçalan provides treatment across a wide range in the field of orthopedics and traumatology.

🦴 Arthroscopic Surgery

Arthroscopic surgery is one of the minimally invasive (closed) surgical methods used in the diagnosis and treatment of joint diseases. A camera (arthroscope) is inserted into the joint through small incisions, allowing detailed visualization of joint structures.

Knee Arthroscopy

A modern surgical method used in the diagnosis and treatment of many knee conditions. Cartilage damage, ligament injuries, and meniscus problems can be treated with arthroscopic methods.

  • Meniscus tears
  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) injuries
  • Knee cartilage damage
  • Intra-articular loose bodies

Shoulder Arthroscopy

Allows closed-method treatment of shoulder injuries and diseases. Problems in the shoulder joint can be treated with smaller incisions.

  • Shoulder dislocations and recurrent dislocation
  • Rotator cuff tears
  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • Shoulder cartilage damage

Ankle Arthroscopy

A modern surgical method for diagnosis and treatment of ankle conditions. Provides effective results especially in sports injuries.

  • Ankle ligament injuries
  • Cartilage damage
  • Ankle impingement syndrome
  • Intra-articular loose bodies

Advantages of Arthroscopy

  • Smaller surgical incisions
  • Less pain
  • Faster recovery process
  • Shorter hospital stay
  • Faster return to daily life and sports

🦾 Prosthetic Surgery (Hip and Knee)

Hip and knee prosthetic surgery is an effective treatment for patients suffering from severe joint damage, osteoarthritis, and various orthopedic conditions causing pain and limited movement.

Hip Prosthesis

  • Hip osteoarthritis
  • Hip fractures
  • Rheumatic joint diseases
  • Congenital disorders in the hip joint

Knee Prosthesis

  • Advanced knee osteoarthritis
  • Shape deformities in the knee joint
  • Rheumatoid arthritis and other rheumatic diseases
  • Joint damage after trauma
  • Severe knee pain and limited movement

Advantages of Prosthetic Surgery

  • Reduction of chronic joint pain
  • Increased joint mobility
  • Ease in daily living activities
  • Improved walking and mobility
  • Significant improvement in quality of life

🏃 Sports Medicine

Sports medicine deals with diagnosis and treatment of muscle, ligament, tendon, and joint injuries occurring during sports or daily physical activities.

Sports Injuries

  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) injuries
  • Meniscus tears
  • Shoulder dislocations and ligament injuries
  • Rotator cuff tears
  • Ankle ligament injuries
  • Muscle and tendon tears
  • Sports-related fractures and dislocations

Treatment Methods

  • Arthroscopic (closed) surgical methods
  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction
  • Meniscus repair or meniscectomy
  • Shoulder stabilization surgeries
  • Tendon and ligament repairs
  • Sports rehabilitation programs

Specialized treatment protocols are applied for safe return of athletes to sports.

🩹 Trauma Surgery

Trauma surgery encompasses surgical and non-surgical treatment of fractures, dislocations, ligament injuries, and soft tissue damage resulting from falls, accidents, or sports injuries.

Treated Conditions

  • Bone fractures
  • Joint dislocations
  • Sports injuries
  • Ligament and tendon injuries
  • Muscle and soft tissue damage
  • Orthopedic injuries after traffic accidents

Treatment Methods

  • Fraction fixation (plate, screw or rod applications)
  • Closed or open fracture surgeries
  • Arthroscopic surgical methods
  • Ligament and tendon repairs
  • Rehabilitation and physical therapy processes

Rapid intervention and correct treatment planning are of vital importance in trauma surgery.

🖐 Hand Surgery and Microsurgery

Hand surgery and microsurgery is an advanced surgical field dealing with diagnosis and treatment of diseases, injuries, and congenital conditions affecting the hand, wrist, and forearm.

Treated Conditions

  • Hand and wrist fractures
  • Tendon lacerations and injuries
  • Nerve injuries
  • Finger amputations and limb avulsions
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
  • Trigger finger
  • Hand cysts and tumors

Microsurgery Methods

  • Reattachment of severed fingers or limbs
  • Nerve repairs
  • Vessel repairs
  • Treatment of complex hand injuries
  • Tissue graft and reconstructive surgery

Microsurgical techniques performed under a microscope make it possible to repair very small structures.

📏 Limb Lengthening Surgery

Limb lengthening surgery is based on the principle of controlled bone elongation. It can be applied in treatment of congenital or acquired leg shortness, leg length discrepancies, and some deformities.

Treated Conditions

  • Leg length discrepancy (difference 2 cm and above)
  • Congenital bone shortness
  • Bone shortness after trauma
  • Growth disorders after bone infections
  • Hormonal disorders (acromegaly etc.)

Surgical Methods

  • Limb lengthening with internal devices
  • Limb lengthening with external devices
  • Complex deformity corrections
  • Reconstructive surgery

This treatment method significantly improves patients' quality of life and psychological confidence.

📐 Deformity Surgery

Deformity surgery is a special orthopedic field aimed at correcting congenital or acquired bone shape abnormalities, specializing in treatment of leg shortness, curvatures, and joint deformities.

Treated Deformities

  • Ovalık (genu varum) - Outward angulation of knees
  • Kavalık (genu valgum) - Inward inclination of knees
  • Congenital hip dislocation
  • Ankle deformities (pes planus, cavus)
  • Shape disorders after bone infections

Treatment Approaches

  • Osteotomy (bone cutting and realignment)
  • Deformity correction with Ilizarov technique
  • Complex reconstructive surgery
  • Joint replacement

In deformity surgery, long-term planning and patient follow-up are important. Patients achieve lasting results after treatment processes lasting several months.

🩺 Diabetic Foot Surgery

Diabetic foot surgery is a special orthopedic field dealing with diagnosis and treatment of foot problems in diabetic patients caused by nerve damage (neuropathy) and circulatory disorders.

Treated Conditions

  • Diabetic foot ulcers
  • Cracked toe bones
  • Bone infections (osteomyelitis)
  • Charcot foot disease
  • Pressure sores and necrosis

Treatment Approaches

  • Debridement (dead tissue cleaning)
  • Antibiotic treatment
  • Conservative bone surgery
  • Reconstructive and repair surgery
  • Tissue graft and microsurgery

In diabetic foot treatment, early intervention is crucial. With early diagnosis and correct treatment, amputation risk can be significantly reduced.

🦴 Bone Tumor Surgery

Bone tumor surgery deals with diagnosis and treatment of benign or malignant tumors in bones. This area is also known as oncologic orthopedics.

Treated Tumors

  • Benign bone tumors (osteomyelitis, osteoid osteoma)
  • Malignant bone tumors (osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma)
  • Myeloma
  • Metastatic bone lesions

Treatment Approaches

  • Limb salvage surgery
  • Prosthesis replacement
  • Multidisciplinary approach with radiotherapy and chemotherapy
  • Reconstructive surgery

In bone tumor treatment, early diagnosis and correct treatment planning are vital. The best results are achieved through multidisciplinary team work.

Our Treatment Approach and Principles

We use the latest technology and treatment methods continuously updated based on scientific developments. Our goal is to perform surgical interventions through minimally invasive (closed) methods as much as possible.

We create individualized treatment plans for each patient, taking into account their medical conditions, lifestyles, and expectations. Through trust-based relationships with our patients, we work in full collaboration during treatment processes.

Patient Care Principles

  • Priority Patient Safety: We maintain the highest level of patient safety in all surgical procedures.
  • Informed Consent: Detailed information is provided before each treatment, and patients are informed about risks and alternatives.
  • Open Communication: We listen carefully to our patients' questions and provide clear explanations in understandable language.
  • Continuous Follow-up: Through regular check-ups during the post-surgical period, we closely monitor the healing process.
  • Team Collaboration: Working in coordination with nurses, physiotherapists, radiologists and other health personnel.

Technology and Equipment
